Understanding Pulmonary Conditions

Healthy Lungs (Normal)

A normal chest radiograph displays clear black lung zones representing fully inflated, healthy air-filled alveoli, with no opacities, consolidations, or suspicious tissue cavities.

Expected Indicators

  • Clear Airway
  • Normal Respiration
  • Full Expansion
  • No Congestion

Pneumonia Infection

An inflammatory condition of the lung parenchyma, where alveoli fill with fluid or pus. Radiographs show localized or diffuse cloudy white patches, known as consolidations.

Key Symptoms

  • High Fever
  • Sharp Chest Pain
  • Productive Cough
  • Short Breath

Tuberculosis (TB)

A highly contagious bacterial infection caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Radiographic markers include upper lobe dense nodules, scar tissues, and fibrous cavitary lesions.

Key Symptoms

  • Bloody Cough
  • Night Sweats
  • Weight Loss
  • Fatigue
